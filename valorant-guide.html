Valorant Aim Guide

Valorant rewards smart aim, strong positioning, and good decision-making. This guide covers the basics that help new players improve faster.

Back to Guides

What Valorant Is

Valorant is a tactical shooter where aim matters, but teamwork, utility, and positioning matter just as much. Players win by combining mechanics with smart choices every round.

Crosshair Placement

Keep your crosshair at head level as you move around the map. This reduces the amount of adjustment you need when someone appears and helps you react faster.

Stop Before You Shoot

In Valorant, accuracy drops hard while moving. A huge beginner mistake is trying to run and shoot at the same time. Stop, shoot, then move again.

Use Utility With Purpose

Do not waste abilities just because you have them. Use flashes, smokes, and other tools to take space, block vision, or force enemies into bad positions.

Best Beginner Tips

  • Play fewer agents at first and learn them well
  • Hold cleaner angles instead of wide peeking everything
  • Communicate enemy positions clearly
  • Focus on consistency before flashy plays

What To Practice First

Work on head-level crosshair placement, stopping before shooting, and learning common angles on each map. Those three things alone can improve your performance a lot.
